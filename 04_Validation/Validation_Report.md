# Validation Report

## 1. Objective

The objective of this validation study was to establish a reliable ChemSep model capable of reproducing the extractive distillation process reported in the reference literature.

Since the published study did not provide all operating specifications required for exact reproduction, the missing process parameters were determined through engineering assumptions and systematic sensitivity analysis. The validated simulation model was then used as the baseline for dataset generation and subsequent AI-based process optimization.

---

## 2. Validation Strategy

The validation was performed in two stages.

First, sensitivity analyses of both the extractive distillation column (Column 1) and the solvent recovery column (Column 2) were carried out to identify the operating variables that significantly influence process performance.

The sensitivity studies established physically meaningful operating ranges for the unknown parameters reported in the literature.

Using these operating ranges, sixteen validation cases were generated by varying only the parameters that had the greatest influence on process behaviour:

- Column 1 Boilup Ratio
- Column 2 Bottom Flow
- Column 2 Pressure

All remaining operating conditions were kept constant throughout the validation study.

The validation case producing the closest agreement with the literature while maintaining stable convergence of both columns was selected as the final validated operating condition.

---

## 3. Validation Criteria

A simulation case was considered valid only if it satisfied all of the following requirements.

✓ Successful convergence of both distillation columns

✓ Physically realistic temperature profiles

✓ Ethanol product purity close to the published literature

✓ High ethylene glycol recovery

✓ Stable condenser and reboiler duties

✓ No abnormal phase behaviour

Only cases satisfying all of these criteria were considered during the validation process.

## 6. Comparison with Literature

The validated simulation successfully reproduced the overall separation behaviour reported in the reference study.

The predicted ethanol purity of 96.15 wt% closely matches the value reported in the literature, while the ethylene glycol purity at the bottom of the solvent recovery column reached 99.51 wt%, demonstrating effective solvent recovery.

Although the reference paper does not report every operating parameter or energy duty required for exact numerical comparison, the simulated process reproduced the expected trends in purity, recovery and operating temperatures.

The successful convergence of both columns further confirms the physical consistency of the developed process model.

---

## 7. Discussion

The published research paper does not provide complete operating specifications necessary for exact reproduction of the simulation.

Consequently, several engineering assumptions were required during model development.

Rather than selecting arbitrary operating conditions, the missing parameters were systematically determined through sensitivity analysis and engineering judgement.

The validation study therefore focused on reproducing the overall process behaviour rather than obtaining identical numerical values for every process variable.

The resulting simulation showed excellent agreement with the published separation performance while maintaining physically realistic temperatures, stable convergence and practical operating conditions.

This validates the developed ChemSep model for subsequent optimization studies.

---

## 8. Conclusion

The developed extractive distillation model was successfully validated against the published literature.

The selected operating conditions produced stable convergence in both columns while achieving ethanol purity and ethylene glycol recovery comparable to those reported in the reference study.

The validated simulation was therefore accepted as the baseline process model and used for dataset generation, machine learning model development and AI-based operating condition optimization.
